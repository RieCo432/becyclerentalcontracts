Colin Ries

In August 2021 I needed to have my gears adjusted. Alistair suggested I go to Becycle with him. It was a nice summer day and after my gears had been adjusted, I decided that I did not want to leave the workshop. I asked if there was anything I could do, and so I was given my first task as a volunteer: reassembling a wheel hub, replacing a spoke and truing the wheel. With many long-term volunteers leaving over the months that followed, I assumed the role of deposit bearer. Later, I would also assume the role of managing the bank account. More recently, Becycle has decided to become a registered charity. As a result, we realised that we would need to step up our record-keeping game and go digital. This prompted me to develop the Becycle Management Experience, BMX for short. Sorry, I just had to make this pun. Knowing that my days in Aberdeen would come to an end, I hereby give you an easy way to contact me in case anything breaks.
